💡 Key Insights

Data-driven findings from 201 countries

📈

Higher GDP = Lower Inequality

Countries with higher GDP per Capita consistently show lower Gini scores. Norway (Gini 26), Denmark (Gini 28), and Sweden (Gini 28) are the world's most equal nations — all with GDP above $50,000.

Page 2 — Scatter Plot
🔍

Inequality Drives Crime

Brazil (Gini 53) and South Africa (Gini 63) both top the homicide rankings. A 1-point increase in Gini Index leads to a measurable increase in homicide rates globally.

Page 3 — Homicide Analysis
💼

Unemployment Fuels Inequality

South Africa has 32% unemployment AND the world's highest Gini of 63. Countries with higher unemployment consistently show higher inequality — proven in Page 4 scatter plot.

Page 4 — Impact Analysis
🔮

Global Inequality is Declining

From 43 in the 1990s to 40.60 today — global inequality has been falling. Power BI forecast predicts it will reach 35–38 by 2040 if SDG goals are achieved.

Page 5 — Forecast 2040
🌍

Africa Most Unequal Region

Sub-Saharan Africa consistently ranks as the most unequal region. South Africa (63), Namibia (59), Botswana (53) lead globally — a legacy of historical wealth concentration.

Page 1 — Global Overview
🎯

SDG Goals Lagging Behind

Latin America and Sub-Saharan Africa are furthest from achieving SDG Goals 11 and 16 — directly linked to their high inequality levels and low economic participation rates.
